Symbolism of Beauty Pageants

She joined the pageant that symbolizes both validation and oppression. Trisha made it to be the winner. The question she answered was "If you were given the chance to live again, what would you choose and why?” Then she answered "If I were to die and be blessed to live again, I would choose to be me, because I am the only person who can fulfill what I need to do... so I would choose, nobody, nobody, nobody but me!" This moment encapsulates her journey of self-acceptance and resilience. It emphasizes that even if the world is against you, you have yourself that always accepts and loves you because no one in this world can love you the way you love yourself. Despite societal pressures, discrimination, and personal hardships, she asserts that her unique identity as a transgender woman is her most valuable asset. And Trisha’s answer is a profound affirmation of self-acceptance, resilience, and the intrinsic worth of authentic identity. It conveys that despite life's hardships and societal expectations, the most vital choice is to remain true to oneself.

At last, the one clip that Trisha’s bond with her best friend Barbs (Christian Bables) and her adopted daughter Shirley Mae contrasts with her estrangement from her biological family. Cultural and Societal Impact

Die Beautiful has significantly contributed to Filipino cinema’s representation of LGBTQ+ stories, challenging stereotypes and fostering empathy. It advocates for a more inclusive society where love and acceptance transcend biological ties and societal expectations.

Conclusion

Overall, the film suggests that family is not defined by blood but by the love and commitment individuals share, emphasizing the importance of creating supportive and inclusive communities.
