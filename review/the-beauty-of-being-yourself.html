The Beauty of Being Yourself: A Reflection on Die Beautiful

Die Beautiful (2016), a Filipino LGBT comedy-drama directed by Jun Robles Lana and starring Paolo Ballesteros, is a film written and created by Jun Robles Lana. Released on October 27, 2016 at the Toronto International Film Festival (TIFF) and on December 25, 2016 at the Metro Manila Film Festival (MMFF), this 120-minute movie, spoken in Filipino and English, comes from the Philippines and explores identity, acceptance, and the struggle to live authentically.

Tracing Trisha’s Journey: Life, Death, and Identity

Die Beautiful is a Filipino LGBT comedy-drama that explores identity, acceptance, and the struggle to live authentically. The film follows Trisha Echevarria, a trans woman portrayed by Paolo Ballesteros, whose life is recounted after her death on the same day she is crowned Binibining Gay Pilipinas. Through a series of non-linear flashbacks, the movie reveals Trisha’s experiences of discrimination, rejection, friendship, and self-expression. Its unique storytelling balances humor with poignant moments while addressing serious issues such as transphobia, familial rejection, and societal expectations.

The Colorful Struggle: Humor, Heart, and Gender Expression

The film opens with Trisha’s funeral preparations, immediately establishing a mix of comedy and emotion. Trisha’s friends meticulously prepare her body, dressing her in glamorous outfits inspired by famous female celebrities. One of the most memorable scenes shows her transformation into Madonna, complete with a sparkling gown and signature style, during which one friend remarks, “She lives her truth in every outfit.” This moment highlights the performative and deeply personal nature of gender expression, reinforcing that identity is both emotional and outwardly visible.

The film does not shy away from the harsh realities of transphobia. In a flashback, Trisha experiences harassment while walking through a public space, where a man mocks her appearance and threatens her safety. Another powerful scene portrays her father publicly scolding Trisha, refusing to accept her as a woman. These moments reveal the emotional struggle faced by transgender individuals, emphasizing the importance of familial acceptance and societal awareness.

Trisha’s relationships with her friends serve as a central theme of the chosen family. During the wake, her friends collaborate to ensure she is celebrated as her true self. These scenes are both humorous and moving, such as when they creatively recreate celebrity looks for each night of her wake. This ritual is not merely comic relief; it demonstrates loyalty, love, and affirmation of Trisha’s identity even after death, underscoring a broader message about the value of support networks in marginalized communities.

Performances and Perspectives: Social Commentary in Action

Die Beautiful successfully combines humor, drama, and social commentary. The screenplay by Rody Vera cleverly balances laughter and sadness, making the audience reflect on issues such as transphobia, gender norms, and self-expression. Paolo Ballesteros delivers an outstanding performance, portraying Trisha as bubbly, funny, and deeply sensitive. His acting earned him the Best Actor Award at the Tokyo International Film Festival (2016) and serves as a powerful representation of transgender experiences in Philippine cinema.

The film also uses culturally relevant symbols, such as beauty pageants, to explore societal validation and the pressures of gender performance. Scenes of Trisha participating in pageants juxtapose public spectacle against private struggles, reinforcing the tension between societal expectations and personal authenticity.

Celebrating Authenticity: Lessons from Die Beautiful

Overall, Die Beautiful is a culturally significant and emotionally resonant film that celebrates identity, resilience, and the importance of acceptance both from oneself and from others. Its message is clear: “Be yourself. Don’t let others bring you down. Stick with who you are and pursue your dreams.” Through its vibrant visuals, creative storytelling, and impactful performances, the movie entertains, educates, and inspires. It is a must-watch for those interested in understanding the complexities of gender, social inclusion, and the Filipino LGBTQ+ experience.
